A lens decides **how a slice of the world is cut and projected onto the sensor**. If exposure is about "how much light," the lens is about "the shape light arrives in."

## Four things a lens decides at once

- **Width of view** — [[focal-length|Focal length]]
- **Sensor coverage** — [[crop-factor|Crop factor]]
- **Background blur** — [[depth-of-field|Depth of field]]
- **Sense of space** — [[perspective-compression|Distance to subject]]

These don't operate independently — **they push on each other**. Going telephoto narrows the angle and shallows the depth at the same time; the same focal length on a smaller sensor gives a different result.

## One-line principles

> **F-number is a ratio.** Same F-number = same brightness across focal lengths.
> **Longer mm = shallower depth.** Counterintuitive but true.
> **Perspective is set by distance.** Not by the lens — by your feet.
> **Crop only narrows the angle; it doesn't make the lens a real telephoto.**
