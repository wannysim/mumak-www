A panel that splits color into **8 channels** (Red / Orange / Yellow / Green / Aqua / Blue / Purple / Magenta) so each can be edited separately. Start here if you've never properly used it.

## Three sliders per channel

Each channel gets three sliders.

| Slider         | What it touches          | Practical effect                           |
| -------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Hue**        | The tone of that color   | Shift red toward orange, or toward magenta |
| **Saturation** | Intensity                | Toward gray ↔ toward vivid                 |
| **Luminance**  | Brightness of that color | That color brighter or darker              |

> Key idea: **adjust one color without touching the others.**

## The four moves you'll reach for most

### 1. Skin — the Orange channel

For most skin tones, **skin lives in the Orange channel** (with some Red).

- **Orange Saturation -10**: pulls skin saturation back for **a more natural tone**. Reduces the over-flushed / over-makeup feel.
- **Orange Luminance +10**: brightens skin for a **clean, healthy look**. Blemishes feel less prominent.
- **Orange Hue slightly -**: pulls overly yellow skin **toward pink**.

Just learning this one move changes portrait edits.

### 2. Sky — Blue / Aqua channels

- **Blue Saturation +**: deeper blue.
- **Blue Luminance -**: darker, richer sky (better contrast against clouds).
- **Aqua too?** Korean skies often contain **Aqua mixed with pure Blue**, so it's worth touching both.

### 3. Foliage / grass — Yellow / Green channels

- When the camera renders leaves too yellow, **Green Hue -** (cooler green).
- **Yellow Saturation -** to mute browning grass; **Yellow Luminance +** to lift autumn leaves.

### 4. Clothing / objects

A red outfit shifted slightly **toward magenta** (Red Hue -) can read more refined, and **slight Saturation -** lets it sit better with the rest of the palette.

## Building a signature tone

Pick one or two channels you tend to touch and a personal look starts to form.

| Look concept          | Common moves                                                   |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Clean / everyday      | Orange Lum +, Yellow Sat -, Blue Lum -                         |
| Vintage / film        | Orange Sat -, Green Hue +, Yellow Hue +                        |
| Cinematic / rich      | Blue Sat +, Orange Sat +, [[tone-curve\|teal & orange curves]] |
| Minimal / desaturated | Overall Sat -10 to -15, fine adjustments via Luminance only    |

## Targeted Adjustment Tool (TAT)

The **little circle icon** at the top-left of the panel. Click it, then click and **drag on a point in the photo** — Lightroom auto-detects the channel and moves its slider.

- Drag up on a face → Orange Luminance rises automatically
- Drag down on a sky → Blue Luminance falls (darker sky)

A way to work HSL **by eye, without memorizing channels**. The fastest entry point for someone learning HSL.

## Common pitfalls

- **Don't push too hard.** Almost every effect lives within ±10. Beyond ±50 colors break or mask edges become visible.
- **Saturation and Vibrance differ.** Vibrance only lifts already-low-saturation colors — it protects skin tones in portraits.
- **Do HSL after [[white-balance|white balance]].** Touch HSL on a wrong WB and everything will need redoing.
