The sequel to [[zootopia-1|Zootopia]]. Honestly exceeded expectations. Formulaic in its plotting — predictable beats, the classic buddy cop clichés — but it works as a genuine tribute to everyone who stayed with the first film.

## Key Themes

- **Marsh Market**: The standout new setting. A district where rejected reptiles live on the margins, avoided by everyone else. What makes it interesting is that even Nick — someone who has survived plenty of his own exclusion — has something close to a phobia about the place. The film doesn't let him be uncomplicated about this, and that tension is worth something.
- **Judy and Nick as a couple**: The film leans into reading their relationship as romantic, and it uses that framing deliberately. The way two people who care about each other still have to work through their differences — and can get stuck, and then suddenly unstuck — is designed to mirror something larger: how societies move through prejudice and how people have to actually do the work. The scene where everything that's been building between them finally breaks open was genuinely moving, and it hit close.
- **A story only a sequel could tell**: The film earns the things it tries to do because [[zootopia-1|the first film]] already laid the ground. Without that, none of the emotional payoff would land. It is not a film that could stand alone, and it doesn't need to be.

## Summary

Predictable structure, but the right kind of fan film — one that uses its extra room to go places the original left closed.
