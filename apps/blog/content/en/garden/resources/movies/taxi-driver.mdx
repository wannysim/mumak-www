A classic American film by Martin Scorsese. First encountered it in a university film course. The New York nightscape and Travis Bickle's isolation linger for a long time.

## Key Themes

- **Loneliness in the crowd**: Travis lives in a city of millions, yet feels profoundly alone. A representative portrait of modern alienation — hard to personally relate to now, but entirely believable from the perspective of Vietnam veterans returning home to a society that had moved on without them.
- **The taxi as a closed world**: Trapped in a narrow, reeking cab inside a vast and indifferent city, Travis's inner world shrinks to match the container he inhabits. The premise itself is what makes the film so compelling: a man whose entire reality has been reduced to the size of a taxi.
- **"You talkin' to me?"**: The mirror scene is the clearest image of the film. Watching someone rehearse confrontation alone, the gradual slide from confusion into something colder — it captures the protagonist's transformation into madness in a way that is hard to shake.

## Summary

What stays is not the violence but the structure: a city that never stops moving, and inside it, one man going nowhere, circling in a box of his own making.
