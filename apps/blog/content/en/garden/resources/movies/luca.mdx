A Pixar animated film. Saw it in theaters in 2021. It moves with the softness of summer, the sea, and friendship — and feels like a fragment of memory turned into a dream.

## Key Themes

- **Two halves of a whole**: Choosing between Luca and Alberto is impossible. The film only works when they are together. Their dynamic is what carries the entire emotional weight of the story.
- **"They're not monsters — they're Luca and Alberto"**: Giulia's father stepping forward and saying those words is the scene that stays with me most. What moves me is the precision of it: not "they're harmless" or "they're good kids," but calling them by their names. And then the two grandmothers stepping out from under their umbrella — the film doesn't promise that everyone will accept them, but makes clear that some people will, and that is enough.
- **The janky Vespa**: A ramshackle scooter held together with enthusiasm and bad ideas. A funny and perfect symbol for the kind of freedom that only exists when you haven't yet been told it's impossible.

## Summary

Small and modest, but full and stylish. Drawn from the director's childhood, it feels like a fragment of memory — specific, a little rough around the edges, and somehow more vivid for it. A quietly beautiful fairy tale.
